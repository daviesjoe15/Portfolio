iOS Golf App

Introduction

ProGolfMe is an app that connects golf coaches with golf players where the players can pay for in-app lessons and performance feedback.

The golf player can video their swing and send it to any golf coach of their choice. The coach will then return a video message giving them pointers. Simple right..!?

The Problems

ProGolfMe required an MVP (minimal viable product) within 3 months and wanted to be capable of generating revenue within 6 months.

At this stage, ProGolfMe had no branding or app designs, just a logo.

Communicating with the user what needed to be done in the app was evidently going to be a challenge.

Research

Using the existing logo, I generated two colour palettes and logo variations. I contacted hundreds of golfers through a golf forum and simply held a poll to see what was the preferred brand style.

Discussions with golf coaches early on revealed that they also wanted access via an app because it would be easier to take and send videos.

Testing

Visits to the local golf club to test prototypes revealed a lack of clarity and disjointed user flows.

At this stage I suggested it would be beneficial to do our work at the golf club to test more frequently with a fail fast approach, this strategy was approved and consequently, we gained deeper insights.

Design

I developed the company branding and with it a cohesive style guide.

I drafted detailed wireframes to achieve a polished product ready for release.

I developed a simple flow that was intuitive and provided a far higher success rate for all age brackets compared with the MVP.

The coaches had demanded an app experience, therefore I was able to strategically design the app to share assets and app architecture. I made the primary colour different so that it was obvious which app you were using.

By simplifying the navigation into three categories, Profile, Chat and Browse Coaches I helped reduce the cognitive load.

I covered the whole app experience from sign-up to payment.

Meticulous design communication between myself and app developers in Ukraine was critical for the successful launch of the app.
