Why a design system?

One of StoryStreams missions is to achieve consistent branding across the product and marketing, and a design system would help distribute a seamless brand experience across both of these areas.

At the core of StoryStream is the development team, they are continuously pushed to meet deadlines as we roll out features. A design system would provide them with the building blocks to make quick work of these features.

Getting StoryStream onboard

I presented the pros and cons of adopting a design system to the company seniors and the entire development team (the largest team who would also have to build it). I explained all the costs and benefits, going into the details on how it would speed up our development time and strengthen our brand.

How do I tackle such a large and integrated project?

It was decided that it would be an ongoing side project and that components would be fleshed out when they became necessary. For example, building the analytics interface presented a great opportunity to introduce the "card" component to break up categories of data yet remain flexible for mobile screens.

Getting started

I started by taking the existing designs for our main screen and identifying all the inconsistencies, for example, the grouped buttons were different from the standalone button design. Adjustments like this are important for the user to trust the UI because familiarity with components means they can better predict the outcome of their actions.

Iconography

Icons can be found throughout our product to add clarity and emphasise meaning. This aids our foreign speaking customers as some words/sentences do not always translate perfectly.

Before the design system, the existing icons varied in quality, thickness and style e.g. mixed rounded edges and square edges.

I chose not to purchase an icon set because we often need to create new icons. I recreated all the existing icons and developed a set of rules to keep our icons consistent and clear.

Using Figma

Using Figma I could place the core brand styles at the root of our master components to create a trickle-down structure where our branding is automatically applied to every design.

I categorised our designs by either Brand, Product, Marketing or Projects, this ensures that nothing unfinished gets built as developers cannot access our Projects area.
