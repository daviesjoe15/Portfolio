Storyboards Redesigned

Introduction

Storyboards are an interactive social media grid that sits on our client's product pages. Our clients use Storyboards to boost engagement times on their websites.

Business goal

Generate more sales by increasing engagement times.

My role

I was asked to redesign Storyboards.

Impact

I lifted the average engagement time from 1.6s to 3.9s.

Process

Using a mix of Google and Marvels design sprint methodology I could take a user centered approach whilst getting my colleagues involved during the most impactful areas.

Business goal

By exercising different research techniques, I was able to learn everything about Storyboards and uncover crucial insights. I have included the main findings for each research method below.

Internal Interviews

  1. Storyboards have not been optimised for use on touch devices.

  2. Our clients are failing to promote the use of the hashtags required to bring in new social content, e.g. #porsche911, resulting in a stifled flow of content.

Analytics

  1. An average of 48% of viewers hovered our grid (2 seconds minimum) but only 8% clicked into an item (social media post).

  2. The percentage of Storyboard views on mobile averages 42% and tablet 11%.

User Observation

  1. All participants scrolled past our Storyboards either before it loaded onto the page fully.

  2. Users found the context stimulating, however, the effort required to click into an item to read it, then click out again did not align with what they wanted, which was to read the next post. It was a dead-end user journey that lacked flow.

Design workshop

  1. When hovering an item, we display the item description. Unfortunately, this means users can read all of the items without clicking into one and as a result, they never see CTA buttons.

  2. Items can be perceived as links to social media.

Problems and Solutions

By solving each problem independently, the corresponding solution could be measured as a single entity, therefore, avoiding any bias. Below I have included three of the major problems and solutions.

1. Increase visisibility • Engagement

Problem

Viewers skim past the Storyboards either because it hasn't loaded in.

Question

How might we make the Storyboard more visible whilst the content loads?

Solution

Apply animation to a simple text message e.g. “#VolvoMoment” and load in image placeholder squares, apply this when it scrolls into the viewport.

Hypothesis

We believe that animating placeholder squares and text message for people scrolling quickly or with slow internet will catch their attention and generate more engagement.

Test

Through testing an interactive prototype we found that all participants stopped scrolling to view the new loading animation.

2. Improve flow • Engagement

Problem

Users were experiencing a lack of flow between each social media items.

Question

How might we create a more seamless journey when interacting with the Storyboard?

Solution

Apply carousel functionality when viewing an item full screen.

Hypothesis

We believe that being able to click through to the next item will create more engagement with the Storyboards.

Test

We measured average engagement times for our most popular Storyboards before and after a carousel was implemented.

Impact

Engagement time increased from 2.3s to 6.4s.

3. CTA visibility • Increase conversions

Problem

Our CTA buttons are not clicked enough because viewers don't click into an item and when they do they often go unnoticed.

Question

How might we get more users to click into items and make our CTA buttons more impactful without detracting from the experience?

Solution

By limiting the item description to one line and truncating it, users are forced to click into an item to read it, therefore, exposing CTA buttons. Applying a delayed fade-in to the CTA button will attract the viewer's attention but not be distracting.

Hypothesis

We believe that showing a preview of the item description and applying animation to the CTA button for users will direct the user towards CTA’s and increase their awareness of them.

Test 1

Calculate the average click-through rate of an item for a single Storyboard, then apply truncated text, monitor the click-through rate and compare.

Test 2

Calculate the average click-through rate for CTA buttons, apply the fade-in animation, monitor the clicks and compare.

Impact

The designs are currently being implemented.
