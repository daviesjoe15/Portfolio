Storyboards Redesigned

Introduction

Storyboards are an interactive social media grid that sits on our client's product pages. Our clients use Storyboards to boost engagement times on their websites.

Business goal:
Generate more sales by increasing engagement times.

My role:
As the only product designer I was asked to redesign Storyboards.

Impact:
I lifted the average engagement time from 1.6s to 3.9s.
This is a big deal for our clients whose north star metric is to increase time on site.

Process

Using a mix of Google and Marvels design sprint methodology I facilitated a user centered approach whilst getting my colleagues involved during the most impactful areas.

The design activities I applied

  1. Understand
    • Internal interviews
    • User observation
    • Google analytics
  2. Define - Workshop
    • Role play persona's
    • Daily routine map
    • Journey mapping
    • Prioritise problems
  3. Ideate - Workshop
    • HMW's
    • Post-it sketches
    • Present ideas
    • Vote concepts
  4. Prototype & Test
  5. Hand off
  6. Measure impact

#1 Capture attention

The problem

People were completely missing our product because it was never loading onto the page in time whilst they were scrolling.

All participants scrolled past our Storyboards before it loaded onto the page fully.

User observation

The solution

We added an animation of the #hashtag used (because we also wanted to increase #hashtag awareness). Through testing an interactive prototype we found that all participants stopped scrolling to view the new loading animation.

2. Improving the flow

The problem

I found that participants became frustrated clicking in and out of content, especially when they weren't interested in an item.

"Its annoying to keep clicking in and out!"

User observation

The solution

We added carousel functionality when viewing an item full screen. During beta we found that average engagement time had increased by 1.9 seconds.

3. CTA visibility

The problem

Our CTA buttons could be clicked more because we found that when people click into an item they often go unnoticed.

Participants list the CTA button last when asked to describe elements on the page.

User observation

The solution

By animating the CTA button in after a small delay we increased awareness. CTA clicks went from an average of 47 clicks per day to 230.
